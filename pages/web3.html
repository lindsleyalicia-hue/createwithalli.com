Web3 101 — Your Starter Pack

01

What is a Wallet?

A crypto wallet doesn't hold coins — it holds the private key that proves you own them. Think of it as a password that proves ownership on a public ledger. Custodial (Coinbase) vs non-custodial (MetaMask, Rabby) is the first real decision you make in crypto.

02

What is a Blockchain?

A blockchain is a database where records can be added but never deleted. Every transaction is verified by thousands of computers simultaneously. No single company owns it — that's what "decentralised" means. Bitcoin was the first; Ethereum added programmability.

03

What is Layer 2 (L2)?

Layer 2s like Base, Optimism, and Arbitrum run on top of Ethereum. They batch transactions together and post a summary to Ethereum — so you get Ethereum's security at a fraction of the cost. Most new crypto activity is happening on L2s right now.

04

What is DeFi?

Decentralised Finance (DeFi) is financial services — lending, borrowing, trading — without banks or brokers. Smart contracts replace the middlemen. You keep control of your assets. The risk is you're also the help desk when something goes wrong.
